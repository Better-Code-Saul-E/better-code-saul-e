Proposal

The topic I have chosen for my final project is the design of a landing page for an e-commerce website. This topic interests me because I strictly shop online and some of the most used websites are for e-commerce platforms. We will continue to expand the digital world; the importance of e-commerce websites is undeniable. The success of these online platforms is heavily dependent on their ability to engage users and provide personalized experiences.

The landing page is the first point of contact for most users of an e-commerce site. It is where customers are introduced to the brand, the products, and the overall shopping experience. A well-designed landing page can be the difference between a user exploring further and leaving the website altogether. Therefore, focusing on creating a landing page that is optimized for user engagement, ease of use, and conversion rates is a timely and relevant topic.

My interest in user experience design has grown, and this project will provide an opportunity to create user-friendly interfaces that improve customer interaction and satisfaction. Designing a landing page that prioritizes aesthetics and functionality will be a challenge.

Reasearch Artifacts

User Research Data

Conducting user research could be one of the steps in creating a user-centered landing page. I would collect data through surveys and interviews with individuals who frequently shop online. By understanding their preferences on e-commerce websites, I will be able to make design decisions that align with user needs. This research will provide insights into preferred layout, color schemes, organization of the content, and the importance of features like recommendations, reviews, and simplified checkout.

Analysis of Existing E-Commerce Websites

I will analyze the landing pages of industry leaders, such as Amazon, eBay, and smaller retailers to identify design patterns, and features that resonate with users. This will help me understand common industry practices, as well as innovative approaches. Comparing and contrasting different websites will also provide insight into what is working in the industry and where there might be room for improvement.

Web Analytics Data

Web analytics data like heatmaps and user interaction, will allow me to understand how users interact with landing pages once they visit. This data will give me specific insights into which areas of the landing page attract the most attention, where users tend to drop off, and which sections are overlooked. This artifact is extremely valuable because it is a data-driven approach to designing experiences.

What I Hope to Learn

I am hoping to gain a deeper understanding of the principles and practices involving the landing page. I want to understand the elements that make a landing page effective. Is it the layout, the clarity of the message, or the visual appeal that grabs attention? How do design choices, such as typography, colors, and images, influence behavior?

I plan to explore how data from research, including surveys and interviews, can guide design decisions. By understanding users' preferences. I want to learn how to create a more personalized experience through user personas, journey mapping, and usability testing.

While conducting an analysis, I will gain insights into what makes e-commerce websites successful. What do major retailers prioritize on their landing pages? What elements do they incorporate to attract, engage, and convert users? This will help me learn how to design with best practices in mind while also thinking creatively about opportunities for innovation.

By the end of the semester, I hope to have developed a strong understanding of websites. I aim to apply this knowledge in a practical and meaningful way. While doing so, I hope to grow my knowledge of design and user experience.
